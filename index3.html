Headache Remedies

Home Remedies

1. Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day. Herbal teas and clear broths can also help.

2. Cold or Warm Compress: Reduces inflammation, numbs the pain, and relaxes tense muscles, respectively.

3. Essential Oils: Peppermint oil – Dilute with a carrier oil and apply to your temples and neck; Lavender oil – Inhale the scent or apply diluted oil to your temples.

4. Ginger Tea: Steep fresh ginger slices in hot water for 10-15 minutes. Add honey if desired.

5. Magnesium-Rich Foods: Eat magnesium-rich foods like almonds, spinach, and avocados or take a magnesium supplement.

6. Acupressure: Apply pressure to the LI-4 point, located between the base of your thumb and index finger, for a few minutes.

7. Caffeine: Drink a small amount of coffee or tea; avoid excessive consumption as it can lead to dehydration and more headaches.

8. Herbal Teas: Chamomile tea, feverfew tea – Steep the herbs in hot water for 10-15 minutes and drink.

9. Proper Rest and Physical Activity: Massage and limit screen time.

10. Aromatherapy: Use an essential oil diffuser with lavender, eucalyptus, or peppermint oil, or inhale the scent directly.

Ayurvedic Treatment

1. Herbal Remedies: Ginger tea, apply peppermint oil to temples, ashwagandha powder or capsules.

2. Coriander Seeds (Dhania): Boil coriander seeds in water, strain, and drink the decoction.

3. Shirodhara: Warm herbal oil is poured over the forehead in a continuous stream. It reduces stress-related headaches.

4. Nasya Therapy: Herbal oils like Anu Taila, Shadbindu Tail are administered through the nostrils.

5. Cinnamon Paste: Make a paste of cinnamon powder and water, apply it to the forehead, and leave it on for 30 minutes before washing off.

6. Clove Paste: Grind a few cloves, mix with water to make a paste, and apply to the forehead. It has pain-relieving properties.

7. Yoga and Pranayama: Child's Pose, Bridge Pose, and Corpse Pose. Alternate Nostril Breathing, Bee Breathing.

Note: Always consult an Ayurvedic practitioner before starting any new treatment, especially if you have underlying health conditions or are taking other medications.

Allopathic Treatment

1. Analgesics: Like Acetaminophen for mild to moderate headaches. Follow dosage instructions.

2. Anti-Inflammatory Drugs: Ibuprofen (Advil, Motrin), naproxen (Aleve), aspirin for reducing pain and inflammation associated with tension headaches and migraines.

3. Triptans: Used for migraine headaches like sumatriptan (Imitrex), rizatriptan (Maxalt), and eletriptan (Relpax) work by narrowing blood vessels and reducing inflammation.

4. Antidepressants: Such as amitriptyline(Elavil), can be effective in preventing migraines and chronic tension headaches.

5. Cluster Headaches: High-flow oxygen therapy and specific medications like sumatriptan can be effective during a cluster headache attack.

6. Tension Headaches: Over-the-counter pain relievers and stress management techniques are recommended. Muscle relaxants like cyclobenzaprine (Flexeril) may be prescribed.
