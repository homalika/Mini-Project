Basics of First Aid

First aid is the initial help given to a person who is injured or ill. The ABCs of first aid are:

  • Airway: Ensure the airway is clear.
  • Breathing: Check if the person is breathing.
  • Circulation: Check for pulse and control bleeding.

Common Emergencies

Cardiac Arrest: Perform CPR. Call emergency services immediately.

Choking: Use the Heimlich maneuver to dislodge the object.

Burns: Cool the burn under running water and cover with a sterile dressing.

Fractures: Immobilize the injured area and seek medical help.

Bleeding: Apply pressure to control bleeding.

Specific Situations

Child First Aid: Be gentle and seek medical help for serious injuries.

Outdoor First Aid: Be prepared for environmental hazards and carry a comprehensive first aid kit.

Workplace First Aid: Follow safety procedures and have a first aid kit accessible.

First Aid Kit

A well-stocked first aid kit should include:

  • Adhesive bandages
  • Antiseptic wipes
  • Gauze pads
  • Medical tape
  • Scissors
  • CPR face shield
  • First aid manual
